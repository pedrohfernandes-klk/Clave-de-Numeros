What is tax advisory?

Tax advisory is the technical service that frames decisions, transactions or changes of activity from a tax perspective. The goal is to reduce uncertainty, anticipate obligations and allow the client to decide with clear information. It makes sense to request it before important decisions — not after.

How do you choose an accounting firm?

The choice should weigh technical rigour, responsiveness, clarity of communication, experience with your type of activity and the availability to follow the business over time. A good question to ask: "how long, on average, do you take to answer a question?"

When should I ask for management consulting?

When the accounting information already exists but needs to be interpreted to support decisions. It is especially useful in phases of growth, reorganisation, hiring or change of business model.

Which documents are usually needed?

Invoices, receipts, bank statements, expense documents, payroll details, contracts and other documents related to the activity. The exact list depends on the regime, size and type of client. We have prepared a practical article on the subject.

Does Clave de Números support online businesses?

Yes. We support sales through digital platforms, remote service provision and the reconciliation of payment methods. Transactions with customers abroad are analysed case by case, within the applicable VAT framework.

Do you work with clients outside Montijo?

Yes. Our office is in Montijo — Rua Cidade de Ponta Delgada, Loja 136 — but digital processes allow us to support clients all over Portugal, with the same closeness and responsiveness.

What is Certified Accounting?

It is the service of organising, processing and recording a company's accounting information, ensured by a Certified Accountant and subject to periodic statutory filings. It includes meeting legal deadlines and, where applicable, preparing trial balances and financial statements.

What does a trial balance show?

A trial balance lists the balances of all accounting accounts at a given moment, allowing you to check income, expenses, amounts owed to third parties and available funds. It is a working document, useful for following the activity between year-end closings.

How do you read an income statement?

The income statement shows the income and expenses of a period and the final result, net of taxes. The useful reading lies in comparing periods: understanding whether the activity is growing, stabilising or requiring adjustments to the cost structure.

What obligations does a sole trader have?

A sole trader's obligations vary with the regime: issuing receipts or invoices, periodic VAT filings where applicable, Social Security contributions and an annual personal income tax return. The exact obligations should always be confirmed case by case, as they depend on the framework and are subject to change.

What should I prepare before hiring an employee?

Before the first hire, you need to define the contractual relationship, report the admission to Social Security and prepare payroll processing. Employment-law questions requiring legal opinion should be confirmed with a qualified professional.

How can I anticipate the main tax obligations?

Whenever the available information allows it, the main tax deadlines of the year — VAT filings, payments on account, personal or corporate income tax — can be flagged in advance so the business can organise itself without last-minute surprises.
