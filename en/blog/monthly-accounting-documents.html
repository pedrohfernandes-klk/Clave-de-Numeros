Which documents should you prepare for monthly accounting?

Published 10 July 2026 · Clave de Números

Straight answer: every month, your accountant needs the invoices you issued, the invoices and receipts for expenses, the complete bank statements for the period, payroll details (if you have employees) and any new contract or document relevant to the activity. Organised as they appear, these documents take minutes to gather; left for the end of the month, they become a heavy chore.

Sales and income documents

Include all invoices, invoice-receipts and receipts issued in the period, even those that were cancelled. If you work with digital platforms, also include the platform's sales or payment reports, so the amounts can be reconciled.

Expense documents

Supplier invoices, expenses bearing the company's tax number, tolls, fuel, software subscriptions, rent and insurance. Rule of thumb: if you paid for it and it relates to the activity, keep the document — the decision about its tax relevance belongs to the accountant afterwards.

Bank statements

The complete statement of every account used in the activity, from the first to the last day of the period. It is the document that allows everything else to be reconciled and reveals movements still missing documentation.

Payroll details

If you have employees: absences, overtime, admissions, terminations and contractual changes. These details should arrive early in the month, because payroll processing has deadlines of its own.

A routine that works

  • Create one folder (physical or digital) per month and file documents as they appear.
  • Photograph or scan paper documents on the same day.
  • Send everything to your accountant on a fixed date — for example, by the 5th of the following month.
  • If in doubt about a document, send it anyway with a note.
